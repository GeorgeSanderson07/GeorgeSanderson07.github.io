About Me


I’m passionate about filmmaking and have always wanted to tell stories ever since I was young. As a child I would create my own stop-motion Claymation, and Lego animations in an attempt to tell the stories of my favourite video games and movies. This initially led me to learning basic filmmaking techniques and how to edit, eventually leading to my current career aspirations as a professional filmmaker.

Whilst pursuing my career options, I initially wished to work as a Video Game designer and pursued an education in IT and Computer science before moving on from that line of work in favour of filmmaking. Despite this, my time spent in IT gave me experience in an office environment, working efficiently under a heavy workload and communicating with new people from across the globe daily. From this I developed valuable time management and organisation skills, as well as countless hours of teamwork experience.

Since joining university, I have worked on multiple roles on multiple different productions, often simultaneously. In just the first 6 months of my second year I have worked on 5 different productions in multiple roles such as Director, Cinematographer, Editor, Production Designer and Producer. Each production’s team has ranged in size from small groups with 3 or 4 members to larger ones with upwards of 14 other members, which has allowed me to develop strong communication skills, as well as firsthand experience in leadership and organization.

In my free time, I’m an avid card game player, and continually pursue playing Yu-Gi-Oh! Competitively with the community at my local game store. It’s provided many opportunities to travel across the country to play with both members of my local community, and meet new people with a shared common interest. Besides this, I also spend a lot of time organizing Tabletop Game events with my friends, such as Dungeons and Dragons. Running a game like this can take a lot of planning, including not only scheduling but preparing equipment and setting up a space to play the game, so it has been valuable for me to do so to strengthen both my organization and interpersonal skills.
